Terrestrial Habitats

A terrestrial habitat is any natural environment found primarily on land, providing food, shelter, and mates for organisms. Major types include forests, grasslands, deserts, mountains, and arctic tundra, which are shaped by rainfall, temperature, and vegetation. These ecosystems support biodiversity and provide essential functions like carbon storage.
