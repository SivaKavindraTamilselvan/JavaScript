Aquatic Habitats

An aquatic habitat is an environment dominated by water, supporting specialized plants and animals, ranging from freshwater lakes and rivers to saltwater oceans and marine ecosystems. These habitats are characterized by factors like salinity and flow, with organisms adapting through features like gills, streamlined bodies, and waxy coatings.
